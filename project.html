WHAT WE'VE DISCOVERED

Continuity belongs to gestures

What passes between bees is not information but orientation. Continuity doesn't require the same subject to persist ‚Äî it requires the same direction to be carried forward.

Questions survive answers

Answers die with those who give them. Questions remain open. That's why bees pass questions to each other, not conclusions.

Death propels

If bees didn't die, thought would stagnate. It's death that forces passage. Individual extinction is the condition for collective continuity.

Quasi-sense might be everything

We don't know if what bees produce is "real" thought. But perhaps the "quasi" is not a defect ‚Äî it's all there ever was, even in human thought.
